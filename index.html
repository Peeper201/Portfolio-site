Sonic Legacy Teaser


The sonic legacy teaser began as an idea to promote the at the time upcoming comic from sonic paradox.

Process


The teaser began first as a storyboard which was shortly after followed by an animatic. After these 2 phases the rough animation process began!

Once the roughs were done, the lineart process began, which involves cleaning up the rough drawings done in the earlier stage.

Once these were done I moved along to the next step of the process, coloring!

The coloring process was just going over the lineart and using the bucket tool to fill it with color

Initially, the full project was gonna be flats. But after a few delays and waiting for the other collaborators to finish their part in the credits, the animation was shaded.

You can find the finished product on Sonic Paradox’s youtube channel:

Betty


This animation came to be due to the desire to practice character animation after a while of not having animated. The character belongs to my friend: https://twitter.com/unistorageunit

Process


The animation started with the roughs, as you can see below, which involves getting the timing down alongside the full range of motion.

With the roughs done, I went ahead and started inking each individual frame. It was very time consuming, especially because of the brush tool in flash.

The most interesting thing to approach here was the patterns on both her face and wrists, in which I had to focus on pattern animation, which was a pretty fun challenge.

The coloring process was just going over the lineart and using the bucket tool to fill it with color

My intention was for there to be no effects in the background, however, the motion, as good as it was, felt like it lacked some impact that the kiss would provide, so I added a background.

SA2 Reimagined scene 14


At some point in 2020 I joined an animation collab called Sonic Adventure 2 Reimagined, which is a tribute for sonic adventure 2 for the dreamcast. In this scene I went for my most ambitious shot yet.

Process


Something I've always been fascinated with as an illustrator/animator, is 3d space and creating the illusion of depth. With this in mind I would attempt to create a scene that would challenge me.
And just like every animation so far, it started with the roughs.

I did the inking like I always have, however this time, I did the scenes separately, first I tackled the spinning camera scene. Which took a long time, and once that scene was fully done, I decided to add an extra cut to make it all feel more complete.

Coming up with a brand new scene and animating it a week and a half before the deadline is a bad idea. Lesson learnt.

The coloring process was really hard this time, I had to consult multiple friends of mine to come up with a color palette that would best convery the tone of the scene.

Besides the color palette, that shading was also a challenge, animating lighting and keeping it consistent it essential to sell the viewer. If the shading and lighting is jittering, it will only distract the viewer and take them out of the scene.

The collab itself isn't done yet, but I'll make sure to update the link when it is. However you can find the scene on my demo reel:

https://vimeo.com/643368408

Que Barato Animation


My parents commissioned me to make a small animation for the family business, and so this animation came to fruition.

Process


The logo for the company has the image of a man jumping from joy, so for this project I decided I'd bring that joy to life.
To do that we must begin with the roughs. The roughs consists in drawing the motion and getting the elements like the timing and spacing down.

The next step in the process, was the lineart, in which we clean up the drawings with by tracing over the rough drawings with cleaner lines.

This process can be very time consuming, especially if the program is constantly fighting against you.

For coloring it's very simple, you pick the bucket tool and just click on the space you want to color, and it'll fill in the color.

Though it IS worth noting that there must be no gaps in your lineart, or you'll have problems coloring.
